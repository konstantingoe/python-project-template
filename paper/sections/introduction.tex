\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
Modern machine learning systems increasingly rely on probabilistic reasoning under uncertainty, yet the interplay between model expressivity and inferential tractability remains poorly understood.
In this work we revisit this trade-off through the lens of \emph{structured latent-variable models} and propose a unified framework that subsumes several prior approaches as special cases.

Our contributions are threefold.
First, we formalize the notion of \emph{conditional structural identifiability} and provide sufficient conditions under which it holds (Section~\ref{sec:methodology}).
Second, we derive a scalable variational estimator with provable convergence guarantees.
Third, we validate the approach empirically on both synthetic and real-world benchmarks (Section~\ref{sec:experiments}), demonstrating consistent improvements over strong baselines such as those of \citet{einstein} and \citet{latexcompanion}.

The remainder of the paper is organized as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:methodology} introduces our methodology and theoretical results.
Section~\ref{sec:experiments} reports the empirical evaluation.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes and discusses limitations.
