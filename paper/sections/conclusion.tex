\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
We presented a framework for structured latent-variable modelling under conditional structural identifiability, together with a scalable variational estimator and supporting theory.
Empirical results on synthetic and real-world data confirm that the approach yields meaningful gains over strong baselines.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Our identifiability guarantees rely on assumptions—most notably, sufficient variability of the conditioning variables—that may not hold in every applied setting.
Extending the analysis to weaker assumptions, and to fully unsupervised regimes, is an interesting direction for future work.

\paragraph{Future work.}
Promising avenues include (i) extending the framework to non-stationary time-series data, (ii) characterizing finite-sample identifiability in the under-parameterized regime, and (iii) integrating the estimator with downstream causal-inference pipelines.
